Glossary

Medium refers to materials artists used to produce an artwork.

Pigment is a substance contained different ingredients that give color and other properties to paints

Primary Colors are red, blue, and yellow. Each of these colors can't be created by other colors.

Secondary Colors are combinations of two primary colors. Mixing red and blue creates purple. To make green, mix blue and yellow. Orange is a combination of red and yellow.

Value is a quantity of how much light oil paint reflects.

Opaque means how many light paints allow passing through them. When the higher concentration of pigment used is added to paints, paints become harder to see through.

Transparent is a quality of being able to see through paints. It's the opposite of the quality of opaque.

Oxidation is a process where oils in coatings become hardened by reacting with the oxygen in the air as they are drying.

Binder is a substance that binds pigments together in paint.

Thinner is a solvent used to thin or reduce thickness of oil based paints.

Tempera is a fast-drying type of paint that uses egg yolk as a binder.

Early Renaissance was a period that transforms the Middles Ages into Modernity. It was when the Florentine artists emerged and became very influential for people around them; they impacted many people to experiment and dig in more knowledge of art and other studies.

Panel Painting is a flat painted surface of a or more pieces made of wooden.

Post-Impressionism was an art movement, occurred after Impressionism, which focuses on emotional, symbolic, and spiritual elements. It went beyond the limits of Impressionism. It also has a style of having exaggerating strokes of a painting.

Surrealism is a style that explores beyond reality; it seeks many different aspects of imagination and fantasies. it rejects rationality, and it exaggerates the reality which is called surreality

Expressionism mostly focuses on emotional and spiritual elements, and its goal is to draw emotions and feelings out of a viewer.

Symbolism is an art style that has elements that symbolize an abstract idea; it may allow viewers to have several different interpretations of an artwork.

Cubism is an abstract art style; it focuses on making all objects or figures geometric and makes you make out an image of an artwork that does not have perfect outlines of objects or figures.
