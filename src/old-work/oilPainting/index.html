Oil Painting

What is this Medium

Oil paint is mixed with pigment, binder, and thinner. It's different from most types of paint such as tempera, watercolor, or acrylic paint because those mediums are dried by evaporation. It takes oil paint a long time to dry because its drying process is from oxidation. There are various types of oil used for oil paint, and each of them creates a different look and dries at a different speed. The most popular type is linseed oil because it dries faster.

Why is the Medium Used

Their durability and steadfast color are excellent, and there are a handful number of paintings that have been preserved well for over 500 years. They also can be restored.

Since they are slow to try, artists can continue painting and improving their paint for much longer than other types of paint. Because of its longevity, they can blend paints more and make several layers after drying paint for a bit several times.

Oil paint also allows artists to create various richnesses of color, shades, and tones. It also enables them to create either or both opaque and transparent effects depending on how much oil and they put on their paint.

Oil Painting still Stands

This medium has been used since ancient times, and many artists still make oil paintings today. Oil painting is not simply to show an artist's extraordinary skill. The purpose of creating an oil painting is various for each artist. In ancient times, many artists use this medium to illustrate religious stories or those from mythologies. Some of them also painted to show a scenario of history since there were no social media or technology to keep history. As time passes by, more artists use this medium to express their emotions through tones of oil colors they use. Some of them create oil paintings for the purpose of allowing viewers to interpret the meaning or symbol of their artworks. Today, many painters sell their artworks to people who admire the beauty of this medium and use them as decoration for indoor.
