History

Who Invented Oil Paint

While many people believe that the van Eyck brothers invented oil paints in Northern Europe in 1420 before oil paints became popularly used and elevated by the famous painters in the Northern Renaissance, the inventor of oil painting is unknown, but oils had been added to paint mixtures before oil paint became a medium. The earliest known paint with oil is recorded from as early as the 11th century. Theophilus Presbyter's work was identified as a paint used linseed oil with lime and the work was dated to about 1125. There is a painted crucifix with evidence of oils being used from the ancient stave church at Hemse, Sweden was created in about 1180. At Tingelstad in Norway, there is a painted altar in the church that was believed to be used with oil, and it was dated to about 1300. Since there are some different shreds of evidence that show oil paints existed before the van Eyck brothers' time, scientists and researchers are still not able to find the original inventor of oil paint.

How did Oil Painting Develop

The development of oil paint has been identified in the Early Renaissance in Northern Europe and Italy. The artists from these areas had been experimenting with paint using different techniques and materials, and they found that using oil as a pigment binder was better than tempera. Tempera was the main panel painting before oil paint. Eventually, oil paint became more preferred. However, some artists like Raphael and Leonardo da Vinci continued to use the combination of these mediums.

Some Famous Oil Painters

Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo da Vinci was born in Anchiano, Italy, in 1452, and he had been apprenticed with several masters to learn various fields of art such as drawing, painting, and sculpting is one of the famous painter known by almost everyone. He analyzed human anatomy deeply and landscapes which had helped him to be skilled at creating famous artworks such as Mona Lisa and The Last Supper.

Michelangelo

In The Sistine Chapel, there is the Creation Of Adam on the ceiling, which is admired by many people, was painted by Michelangelo. He was born in Florence, Italy, in 1475. Though he identified himself as a sculptor, he had created some well-known paintings.

Vincent van Gogh

Vincent van Gogh is a Dutch artist born in Zundert, Netherlands, in 1853. He's one of the greatest post-impressionist who used striking color and brushwork. He was inspired by Rembrandt, so he was so fascinated by painting. Even though he had never sold one of his artwork, his Starry Night was well known by almost everyone.

Salvador Dali

This artist who was born in Figueras, Spain, in 1904, is known for his Surrealism. Salvador Dali embraced the "greater reality" of the human subconscious, so he painted his works based on his hallucinates which made his artworks so unique, strange, and disturbingly calm. One of his famous artwork is named The Persistence of Memory.

Edvard Munch

Edvard Munch is an expressionist painter born in Norway in 1863. Impressionists had some important influences on his works. He showed powerful emotions in his paintings as he carefully chose rich colors to express them. The style of some of his works is Symbolism. Many people know his famous painting titled The Scream which was based on his own experience of a piercing scream on a walk after his two companions had left him.

Rembrandt van Rijn

Rembrandt van Rijn was born in Leden, Netherlands, in 1606. After he left the university to study painting with a master, Jacob Issacsz van Swanenburgh, and when he returned to his homeland, he quickly became a well-known painter and portraitist. He's known for his The Night Watch and Man with a Sheet of Music.

Johannes Vermeer

Johannes Vermeer is a Dutch artist who was born in Delft, Netherlands, in 1632. He had traveled to Italy and other countries to be exposed by different styles of painting, but Rembrandt's artworks had impacted his style of painting. Most of his artworks are based on biblical and mythological scenes, but he then painted more scenes of daily life. He's best known for Girl With A Pearl Earring

Pablo Picasso

This Spanish painter was born in Malaga, Spain, in 1881. His family encouraged him to become an academic painter, so Pablo Picasso went to Madrid to study art. Later, he discovered an interesting style of painting in Paris, and he was inspired by the artworks of van Gogh because of his brilliant colors. Piccaso is best known for a Cubist painter who had created numerous different pieces of art. Some of his artworks are Guernica and Old Guitarist.
