Graphical and Interaction Design

  • Added background images to the headers by using javascript.
  • Added a horizontal navigation bar that has four tabs, and two of the tabs have sub-sets of tabs that appear when you hover the tab. That allows you to jump from a page to any other page within the website.
  • The bold tab helps you to know what page you are currently on.
  • The background of a tab changes when you hover over the tab.
  • The layout of the navigation is responsive to the change in the screen size. However, I am planning to make a menu tab that collapses all the tabs (for smartphones and tablets) unless the user clicks on the menu tab to view the tabs
  • The body is also responsive to the screen size by changing its margins
  • Added a fading feature to the tab to make the website look more appealing.
  • To beautify the headers a bit, I added a linear gradient color to each of them.
  • The images in the body are floating either on the right or left side, and the text is wrapping around them.
  • I used just one color scheme, excluding the light gray and gray color that I used for the font and body's background, for all the web pages (generated by Coolors). It has five HEX values; the values can be found in the stylesheet for this website.

Content

  • The layout of the content has four parts; there are the header, navigation, body, and footer sections.
  • The footer has links to the resources and documentation pages.
  • The body may and may not contains a few parts (the div tags).
  • The map of Henan, China is shown on the first page, Introduction
  • The citations for the images are on the resources page under the image section and the figcaptions are clickable. You can click on the figcaption to see its source.
